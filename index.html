About Me

I’m interested in decoding disease biology through the lens of data, especially by integrating multiple data layers like multiomics, medical records, and imaging data to identify predictive markers or risks for diseases like dementia, Type 1 diabetes, and cardiovascular disorders. I’m particularly drawn to multimodal data integration and machine learning to solve complex biological problems in a meaningful way.

Right now, at SCFBio, I primarily work at the intersection of Computational Biology, Biophysical Chemistry, and Artificial Intelligence under the supervision of Prof. B. Jayaram, I’m co-developing a Genome Workbench—an attempt at building a universal, biophysical fingerprinting-based characterization framework for identifying and predicting genomic elements like promoters, enhancers, gene boundaries, and exon-intron junctions in eukaryotes. My previous work at the lab involved the biophysical profiling of genomic elements across four kingdoms—Animalia, Plantae, Fungi, and Protista—which revealed distinct signals that could guide genome annotation across organisms. As part of my Master’s dissertation, I also contributed to developing ChemEXIN, a tool for predicting exon-intron boundaries based on DNA’s physicochemical properties.

In parallel, at the Mineral Nutrition Laboratory, in collaboration with Dr. Renu Pandey, I’m leading a genome-wide study to identify P1BS motifs in rice promoter regions. I developed a custom motif-finding pipeline using Bash scripting and performed RNA-seq-based expression analysis under abiotic stresses like drought, salinity, heat, and cold.

Before that, at the Computational Intelligence and Bioinformatics lab, I also conceptualized and conducted a gene-to-drug, molecular dynamics simulation-based study that identified a single small molecule targeting multiple key genes (APP, BACE1, APOE, GSK3B, MAPT, PSEN1) implicated in Alzheimer’s disease.

Across all of these projects, one constant has been my relationship with data—I’m always on the lookout for new datasets to integrate, analyze, and learn from. I genuinely believe that no dataset is ever wasted. Some just take a little longer to speak.

Topics of interest:

  • Integrative analysis of multimodal datasets (genomics, transcriptomics, medical records, imaging) for disease risk and biomarker prediction
  • Machine learning and deep learning approaches for predictive genomics and functional element classification
  • Genome-wide association studies (GWAS) and downstream integration to identify diagnostic and prognostic markers
  • Neurogenomics and disease progression studies, with a focus on dementia, Alzheimer’s, and other complex disorders
  • Data-driven research design, emphasizing dataset integration, and interpretability across biological layers
  • Spatial and single-cell -omics to decode gene regulation at the cellular resolution

I graduated with a B.Sc. in Life Science (Botany, Chemistry, Zoology) at the University of Delhi in 2021 and obtained an M.Sc. in Bioinformatics at the Department of Computer Science, Jamia Millia Islamia in 2023.
In my free time, I enjoy reading, writing poetry, and planning my days beforehand.
