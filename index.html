About Me

Hi, I work at the intersection of Computational Biology, Biophysical Chemistry, and Artificial Intelligence under the supervision of Prof. B. Jayaram. Driven by the thrill of solving the complex puzzles within the genome, my passion lies in deciphering the biological mysteries of functional elements within the genome—a quest that often keeps me up at night.
Currently, I’m working at SCFBio on building a Genome Workbench to identify and predict various genomic elements in eukaryotes. My previous work involved the Biophysical Fingerprinting of eukaryotic genomic elements across four kingdoms: Animalia, Plantae, Fungi, and Protista. This research enhanced our understanding of the biophysical mechanisms that govern genomic function and highlighted the potential of these signals as reliable markers for the efficient annotation of genomic elements across different organisms and kingdoms.
I am also deeply intrigued by neurodegenerative diseases, particularly Alzheimer's disease and its progression. I previously conducted a Gene-to-Drug molecular dynamics simulations-based study that identified a single drug candidate targeting a group of key genes (APP, BACE1, APOE, GSK3B, MAPT, PSEN1) implicated in Alzheimer's disease.
Additionally, as part of my Master’s dissertation, I contributed to developing a novel tool for predicting exon-intron boundaries. While Computational Genomics has allowed me to explore the genome beyond the .FASTA and .gtf/.gff formats as a Bioinformatician, I know I still have a long road ahead of me.
